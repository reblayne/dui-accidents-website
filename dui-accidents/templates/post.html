Exploring past fatal drunk driving accidents across the US

Using data from the Fatality Accident Reporting System over 2007-2017

Everyone always says, “don’t drink and drive,” yet drunk driving is an issue widespread in the United States that continues to take tens of thousands of lives each year. These deaths are accidents that can be and should be avoided.

Quick Facts

- In the US, drunk drivers are responsible for 31% of all traffic-related deaths.

- On average, someone in the US is killed every 51 minutes in a drunk driving accident. This means, that 27 people die, on average, because of a drunk driving accident.

- Deaths and damages due to drunk driving accidents cost the US roughly $52 billion per year.

Exploratory Analysis

Across California, men are responsible for 4.8 times as many fatal DUI accidents over the 11-year span compared to women.
Each point represents the aggregated average from 2007-2017.

Unsurprisingly, the number of drunk driving fatal accidents are highest during times when people are typically drinking more – at night, on weekends, during summer, on holidays.

Fatal DUI accidents occur 5 times more frequently on holidays than on regular days of the year.
