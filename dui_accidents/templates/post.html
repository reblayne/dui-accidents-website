Exploring past fatal drunk driving accidents across the US

Using data from the Fatality Analysis Reporting System over 2007-2017

Everyone always says, “don’t drink and drive,” yet drunk driving is an issue widespread in the United States that continues to take thousands of lives each year. These deaths are accidents that can be and should be avoided.

Quick Facts

- In the US, drunk drivers are responsible for 31% of all traffic-related deaths.

- On average, someone in the US is killed every 51 minutes in a drunk driving accident. This means that, on average, 28 people die each day because of a drunk driving accident.

- Deaths and damages due to drunk driving accidents cost the US roughly $52 billion per year.

Exploratory Analysis

Exploratory analysis on factors that contribute to the prevalence of fatal DUI accidents was broken down into 3 questions:

  1. Who is responsible for these accidents?
  2. Where do these accidents occur?
  3. When do these accidents occur?

Note: The following visuals were created using data from the Fatality Analysis Reporting System over 2007-2017. You can find more details on this dataset described in section 1 of the Road Map page.

1. Who?

Across the US, men were responsible for 5.2 times as many fatal DUI accidents over the 11-year span compared to women. In particular, 21-year-old men were the demographic with the most past instances.
Each point represents the aggregated average from 2007-2017.

Whites were involved in significantly more fatal DUI accidents than any other race from 2011-2017. However, this is likely because whites make up the largest percentage of the population.

2. Where?

Texas, California, and Florida (three of the most highly populated states in the country) had the highest number of fatal DUI accidents over the 11-year span.

However, when population density is taken into account a cluster of states in the Midwest region of the country (Montana, North Dakota, and Wyoming) had the highest fatal DUI accident rates per 100,000 occupants.
Population data by state was provided by the United States Census Bureau.

3. When?

Unsurprisingly, the number of drunk driving fatal accidents are highest during times when people are typically drinking more – at night, on weekends, during summer, on holidays.

Fatal DUI accidents occur 5 times more frequently on holidays than on regular days of the year.

Knowing who is responsible and when these accidents are occurring can help us improve upon preventative measures. Down the Road attempts to highlight these contributing factors regionally in the state of California.
